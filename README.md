## Understanding MDMA and Its Legal Status

MDMA, commonly known as ecstasy or molly, is a synthetic drug with stimulant and hallucinogenic effects. It gained popularity in the 1980s for recreational use but has been studied for potential therapeutic benefits in treating PTSD and anxiety under strict medical supervision.

In 2026, the legal landscape remains unchanged: MDMA is illegal for non-medical use worldwide. The DEA classifies it as having no accepted medical use and high abuse potential. Recent FDA trials for MDMA-assisted therapy show promise, but only through approved clinical programs. Attempting to buy MDMA outside these channels exposes you to health risks like dehydration, neurotoxicity, and impure products from unregulated sources.
